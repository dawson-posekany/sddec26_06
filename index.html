Cost-Effective VIS–NIR Spectrometer Using a Linear CCD Sensor

Project Overview

What We're Building

This senior design project focuses on designing, building, and validating a compact and affordable visible–near infrared (VIS–NIR) spectrometer capable of measuring spectral content of light across approximately 400–1000 nm.

The system is built around the TCD1304DG 3648-pixel linear CCD sensor for spectral detection, driven by an STM32 microcontroller for precise timing, signal acquisition, and data handling.

A primary application is supporting photoacoustic imaging (PAI) research by accurately measuring the center wavelength and full width at half maximum (FWHM) of high-power LEDs and laser diodes used for photoacoustic excitation—critical for spectral unmixing and quantitative imaging.

Conventional spectrometers are expensive and bulky. Our design emphasizes low cost, portability, modularity, and ease of use without sacrificing spectral performance, making it suitable for teaching labs and biomedical optics research alike.

Target Specifications

Wavelength Range 400–1000 nm
Spectral Resolution ~1–5 nm
Detector TCD1304DG (3648 px)
ADC Resolution 10–12 bit
Frame Rate ≥ 10 FPS
Interface USB / UART
Power Supply USB or Battery (5V / 3.3V)
Technical Approach

System Architecture

The spectrometer integrates four key subsystems to deliver accurate, real-time spectral measurements in a portable form factor.

🔮

Optical Subsystem

Entrance slit, collimating optics, diffraction grating, and focusing optics arranged to disperse incident light onto the linear CCD sensor.

⚛

Analog Front-End

Dedicated transimpedance amplifier (TIA), DC offset adjustment, and low-pass filtering to maximize signal-to-noise ratio before ADC conversion.

💻

Embedded Firmware

STM32 firmware manages precise CCD timing using hardware timers and DMA, ADC acquisition, data buffering, and host communication.

📈

PC Software Interface

LabVIEW or MATLAB graphical interface for real-time spectral intensity visualization, data storage, and wavelength calibration workflows.

⚖

PCB & Enclosure

Compact PCB using surface-mount components with careful grounding and noise mitigation. 3D-printed mechanical enclosure for portability.

🔍

Wavelength Calibration

Calibration using narrow-line lasers or calibrated LEDs to map CCD pixels to wavelengths, validated against spectral accuracy targets.

Project Timeline

Expected Deliverables

The project spans two semesters, progressing from design and prototyping to full integration and validation.

1

Semester 1 — Design & Prototype

  • Detailed project plan, milestones, and risk analysis
  • Optical layout design and component selection
  • STM32 microcontroller and CCD interface architecture selection
  • CCD timing and readout firmware implementation
  • Analog front-end circuit design and simulation
  • PCB schematic capture, layout, fabrication, and assembly
  • Initial CCD verification and preliminary spectral measurements
2

Semester 2 — Integration & Validation

  • Signal-to-noise ratio and timing stability improvements
  • Firmware optimization for reliable acquisition
  • Full integration of optical, electronic, and firmware subsystems
  • 3D-printed mechanical enclosure for portable operation
  • User-friendly graphical interface for spectral visualization
  • Wavelength calibration with known reference sources
  • Performance validation and final oral presentation with live demo
